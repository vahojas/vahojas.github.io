About

I am a PhD student at Caltech in the Applied and Computational Mathematics option, working with Professor Oscar Bruno.

My research focuses on wave propagation and boundary integral equation methods, with applications to scattering, periodic structures, and target identification.

Before joining Caltech, I completed my MSc at Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile, where I worked with Carlos Pérez-Arancibia and Manuel A. Sánchez on Perfectly Matched Layers (PMLs) for higher-order finite difference schemes.

Current Research

  • Target identification via scattering poles, using time-domain data and spectral estimation methods (ESPIRA, Prony-type techniques).
  • Scattering poles of periodic structures, with emphasis on two-dimensional gratings and their computation through boundary integral formulations.

Publications

  • V. A. Hojas, C. Pérez-Arancibia, M. A. Sánchez, " Reflectionless discrete perfectly matched layers for higher-order finite difference schemes," SIAM Journal on Scientific Computing 46(5), A3094-A3123, 2024.
